The plan for the FPGA digital filter project is to get the whole design correct
in simulation before it ever touches a board. Icarus Verilog compiles the
testbench and design together; GTKWave shows the resulting waveforms.

This matters more for a filter than for most digital logic, because "correct"
here isn't just "the FSM reached the right state" — it's a numerical claim
about frequency response. A simulation lets that claim get checked against a
known input signal before synthesis, timing closure, or an SPI link to an
STM32 enter the picture at all.

More detail on the filter architecture and the SPI control interface will
follow as the design settles — current status is on the [project
page](/projects/fpga-spi-filter).
