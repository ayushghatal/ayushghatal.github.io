A lot of self-study advice says "just pick a good textbook and work through it."
That works once you already know which questions you're trying to answer. Before
that, a single textbook can quietly narrow your sense of what a subject even is.

The structure I've settled on splits every topic into two tracks:

- **A concept-builder** — usually a narrative or semi-popular text that builds
  physical intuition before the notation shows up.
- **A math-builder** — the formal text, worked problems, and the machinery that
  actually lets you compute things.

Reading them in that order means the equations in the math-builder attach to a
picture that's already there, instead of being the first thing you meet.

This post will get updated as the reading list solidifies — right now it's a
living document more than a finished plan.
